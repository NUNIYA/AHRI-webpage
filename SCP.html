Stakeholders Capacity Building Program (SCP)

The Stakeholders Capacity Building Program (SCP) is an AHRI-developed initiative that aims to strengthen the capabilities of key stakeholders involved in rabies prevention and control efforts. The program focuses on providing training, resources, and support to groups such as public health authorities, veterinary professionals, community leaders, and first responders. By enhancing the skills and knowledge of these stakeholders, the SCP helps to improve early detection of rabies cases, ensure timely and coordinated responses, and promote effective vaccination and education campaigns. The ultimate goal is to build a robust, collaborative network that can effectively tackle the threat of rabies and protect both human and animal populations. The SCP is a crucial component of AHRI's broader efforts to address the global rabies challenge.

Rabies Control Network

The Rabies Control Network facilitates global collaboration among public health, veterinary, and community stakeholders. The network amplifies the impact of capacity-building initiatives to strengthen the collective response to rabies outbreaks and advance elimination efforts.

Research and training collaboration

Research and training collaboration brings together diverse stakeholders to jointly conduct research and develop programs. This approach facilitates knowledge exchange, skill development, and institutional capacity building, contributing to enhanced capabilities. Engaging stakeholders aligns priorities and fosters long-term partnerships crucial for addressing complex challenges.
