MHRP: A Collaborative Effort for a Rabies-Free Ethiopia

The "Mobile Health (mHealth) for Rabies Prevention" (MHRP) project is a groundbreaking initiative tackling the critical public health issue of rabies in Ethiopia. Supported by the VDMdRDD projects initiative, MHRP aims to significantly reduce the threat of dog-mediated rabies, a persistent challenge exacerbated by difficulties in vaccine management and limited public awareness.

At the heart of MHRP lies the innovative Web-based Rabies Vaccine Management System (RVMS), launched in September 2024.

The MHRP project is a collaborative effort involving government agencies, healthcare professionals, and community stakeholders. By leveraging technology and fostering partnerships, MHRP strives to create a healthier and safer Ethiopia, free from the threat of rabies.

Key Facts

Rabies in Ethiopia

  • High Mortality Rate: Ethiopia records approximately 2,700 human rabies deaths annually, making it one of the highest in Africa. This high mortality rate positions Ethiopia as the second highest in rabies mortality across the continent, primarily due to the high incidence of dog mediated rabies infections (World Health Organization, 2022; Global Alliance for Rabies Control, 2023).

  • Widespread Risk: Over 10,000 rabies exposure cases are reported each year in Ethiopia, affecting both urban and rural populations. Rabies is a significant public health issue across all regions of the country, with both urban centers and rural areas reporting high exposure rates (Ethiopian Ministry of Health, 2023; Africa CDC, 2022).

  • Underserved Populations: Less than 50% of rabies exposure victims in rural areas receive timely post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP). The delay in receiving PEP, combined with limited access to healthcare facilities, increases the fatality risk, particularly in remote and underserved communities (Journal of Infectious Diseases, 2023; Ethiopian Public Health Institute, 2023).

Challenges

  • Vaccine Access Issues
    There are frequent vaccine stockouts at health facilities, leading to delays in administering life-saving treatment.
  • Lack of Real-Time Data
    The absence of a robust system to track vaccine inventory and demand hinders efficient management and timely resupply.
  • Public Awareness Deficit
    Limited knowledge about rabies prevention and the importance of seeking immediate treatment following exposure contributes to delayed treatment and increased mortality.
  • Geographical Barriers
    The vast and diverse landscape of Ethiopia makes it difficult to distribute vaccines effectively and reach all at-risk populations.

Why One Digital Platform?

Rabies is a serious and often fatal viral disease that affects both animals and humans. It is primarily transmitted through the bite or scratch of an infected animal, typically a dog or wild carnivore. Effectively controlling and preventing the spread of rabies requires a coordinated and comprehensive approach across multiple stakeholders, including public health authorities, veterinarians, animal control agencies, and the general public.

It provides:

  • Centralized data and information management
  • Coordinated response and intervention
  • Public awareness and education
  • Streamlined vaccination and treatment access
  • Improved surveillance and early detection

Sectors Engagement

Successful digital platforms require engagement across multiple sectors and stakeholders. This includes partnerships with government agencies, industry groups, service providers, community organizations, and end-users. By fostering cross-sectoral collaboration, platforms can leverage the specialized knowledge, resources, and networks of diverse participants. This holistic, multi-stakeholder approach is crucial for developing digital solutions that effectively meet the needs of all relevant parties.

Human Rabies Data Tracking

A rabies control platform would track and analyze comprehensive human rabies data, including cases, exposures, treatments, and related factors like demographics and transmission sources. Real-time monitoring and trend analysis help authorities identify high-risk areas, allocate resources, and make data-driven decisions. The platform also integrates community-level data on vaccination, animal populations, and public awareness, supporting targeted interventions to reduce human rabies.
